Your Name

Author:Motoshi Zhuki

Genre:Love

Language:English

Status:Completed

Mitsuha, a high school girl in a rural town, and Taki, a Tokyo boy, mysteriously begin swapping bodies across time and space. As they adapt to each other’s lives, a bond deeper than friendship forms. Their connection is threatened when Mitsuha’s town faces a devastating disaster. Racing against fate, Taki struggles to save her, even as memories fade. Your Name is a breathtaking story of love, destiny, and the invisible threads that connect souls across worlds.

Your Name

There was once a girl named Mitsuha who lived in a quiet little town called Itomori, nestled among mountains and lakes. Her life felt small, bound by tradition, and she often sighed at the routines of shrine duties and country gossip. Every night, she would look up at the stars and whisper a wish: “Let me be a handsome boy in Tokyo in my next life.” And far away, in that same Tokyo she dreamed of, a boy named Taki lived his busy life. He studied hard, worked part-time at a restaurant, and dreamed of architecture. But though his days were full, something felt missing—like a thread tugging gently at his heart.

One morning, both awoke to find the impossible had happened. Mitsuha opened her eyes to skyscrapers, neon lights, and the noise of Tokyo traffic. She looked into a mirror—only it wasn’t her reflection. She was in Taki’s body. And Taki? He woke up to the sound of cicadas and the smell of countryside air… only to find himself in Mitsuha’s body. At first, they thought it was a dream. But it kept happening. One day Mitsuha would live Taki’s life, fumbling through his job, his friends, and his crush. Another day Taki would stumble through Mitsuha’s school, bewildered by the shrine rituals and small-town gossip. To survive, they began leaving notes for each other—scribbled in notebooks, scrawled on arms, even typed into their phones. Slowly, confusion turned to teamwork, and teamwork blossomed into something unspoken.

Though they had never met face-to-face, they grew close. Mitsuha brought warmth into Taki’s life; Taki gave Mitsuha confidence to stand tall. Their lives began to shine brighter because of each other. And then… one day, it stopped. No more switches. No more notes. Only silence. Taki grew restless. He felt her slipping away, like a dream fading at dawn. Desperate, he set out to find her, sketching landscapes from his memory of her world. After a long journey, he discovered the truth—Mitsuha’s town, Itomori, had been destroyed three years earlier by a falling comet. Mitsuha had died that night. The shock was unbearable. But Taki could not let her go. Guided by instinct, he climbed to Mitsuha’s family shrine and drank the sacred sake she had once left behind. In that moment, their souls intertwined once more, and time itself bent.

They met at twilight. There she was—Mitsuha, alive, standing before him. Their hearts raced as they finally saw each other with their own eyes. They spoke quickly, exchanging names, promising never to forget. But twilight ended, and as the light faded, so too did their memories. Taki called out, desperately trying to hold on, but her name slipped away like water through his fingers. Back in her time, Mitsuha awoke with tears in her eyes, but with Taki’s words echoing in her heart. She raced to save her town, convincing her father and friends to evacuate. The comet came, the sky burned—but this time, the people lived. Years passed. Mitsuha and Taki grew into adults, their memories of each other blurred, yet an emptiness lingered in their hearts. They felt like they were searching for someone, though they couldn’t say who. In the sprawling city of Tokyo, their paths crossed again and again—on trains, in streets, on bridges. Until one day, on a quiet staircase, they saw each other clearly. They stopped, breathless. Taki hesitated, then asked softly, “Haven’t we met before?” Mitsuha, her eyes shining, replied, “I thought so too.” And in that moment, the invisible thread that had bound them through dreams, time, and destiny tightened at last. The story of two souls searching across worlds found its beginning… together.
